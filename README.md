#####Body#####

1. The fourth Booke of the third part of the Mirrour of Knight-hood, wherein is declared, the high and noble actes of the sonnes and nephewes of the Noble Emperour Trebacius, and of the rest of the renoumed Princes and Knights, and of the high cheualrie of the gallant Ladyes: wherein is treated of the most cruell warre that euer was in Greece, with the amourous euents, and the end thereof.

    _ The first Chapter. How the Princesse Rosselia of Rome, being deliuered from the Inchantment, desired her father to bee reuenged on Don Heleno, Prince of Dacia; and of that which followed thereof.

    _ CHAP. II. Of that which happened vnto the Prince Poliphebus of Tinacria, with the Damsell which caried him into the kingdome of Samogacia.

    _ CHAP. III. Of that which hapned to the Prince of Tinacria, with a Knight much affected to Celibella, and how he ended the battell for her.

    _ CHAP. IIII. How the Prince of Tinacria neere the Fountaine of three spoutes, met with the braue Eufronisa, and of the battaile which hee fought with two Giants which would haue robbed him.

    _ CHAP. V. Of that which happened to the Prince Claridiano, and to Florisiano of Apulia, in their returne from Lucania.

    _ CHAP. VI. How the Damsell deliuered the ambassade to the King of Lucania, in the behalfe of the Knight of Crueltie, who admitting the same, the matter came to triall.

    _ CHAP. VII. Of the ende of the cruell battaile betweene the Greeke Prince, and the braue Asternio, and of that which more succeeded.

    _ CHAP. VIII. Howe the aduenture of the hatefull Tower, with the libertie of the fayre Linda brides was ended; with the co•ming of the Ambassadours from Rome; and of that which therewith happened to the Ladyes.

    _ CHAP. X. Of the bloody cruell Iusts, betweene the Ladies and the Romane Ambassadours; and of the comming of Claridiano and the gentle Claramante, and what followed.

    _ CHAP. XI. Of the cruell and bloodie battell, fought by the foure Knights.

    _ CHAP. XII. Of the end of this cruell battell, and how the Greekes returned to their Citie, and of what happened vnto them vpon the way.

    _ CHAP. XV. Of the encounter which the Captaines souldiers had with the enemie, about pitching of their Campe.

    _ CHAP. XVI. How the Prince Poliphebus of Tinacria, entred the Castle of the golden Bough, where hee deliuered the most faire Luzela, daughter to the Emperour of Egypt, from enchantment.

    _ CHAP. XVII. How the Prince of Tinacria tooke his way towards Grecia, in companie of the gallant Luzela, and how hee set his sister Rosaluira at libertie, from the power of the Giants, which carried her to Sea.

    _ CHAP. XVIII. Of the bloudie skirmish which was betweene the two Armies, about the taking of certaine heards, and of the comming of the Earle of Modica.

    _ CHAP. XIX. How the Prince Poliphebus of Tinacria came to the Campe, and what hee did for the libertie of the Flemmish knight, with the comming of the Princes of Silepsia, Leobulo, and Claribel, and what with them happened to the Princes of Grecia.

    _ CHAP. XX. What happened to the Knight with the starers, and to Rosabel in the combat: and the end thereof.

    _ CHAP. XXI. How the Princes of Grecia, Claridiano, and Claramante, with the Queene of Lira came to the battell, and what end it had, with that which after followed.

    _ CHAP. XXII. How the Prince of Tinacria sent a Letter of challenge to the Pagans camp, with leaue from the Emperour of Grecia, and of what followed thereof.

    _ CHAP. XXIII. How Poliphebus beganne to maintaine his challenge, and of the perilous battaile which followed.

    _ CHAP. XXIIII. Of the cruell battell betweene Brundusio and Poliphebus, with the comming of the couragious Lindoriano, Prince of Niquea.

    _ CHAP. XXV. Of the cruell battell betweene the two Princes.

    _ CHAP. XXVI. Of the cruell battaile fought betweene the youth Clecbulo, and Poliphebus of Tinacria, with what happened to Flori•arte of Argentaria.

    _ CHAP. XXVII. How the Prince Poliphebus made himselfe knowne to the Emperour his Father, and of the good reliefe which Meridian brought to the besieged, and of the rest which followed.

    _ CHAP. XXVIII. How the Greekes with victorie returned to the Citie, where they made preparation for the field, and how the two youthes were made knowne to their mother.

    _ CHAP. XXIX. How the field battell began, and of the perilous combats and famous actes which therein succeeded.

    _ CHAP. XXX. How the mightie Andronio, Lord of the great Tartaria, came to aide the Greekes, and of the end of the field battel.

    _ CHAP. XXXI. How the Princes of the Campe entred into the Citie, and of the feasts which were made them: and the Marriages, which were contracted for the confirmation of the peace.

    _ CHAP. XXXII. How the redoubted Brauorante required the braue Brufaldoro to performe the challenge past and agreed vpon, and of the cruell battell which was betweene them, with that which followed.
